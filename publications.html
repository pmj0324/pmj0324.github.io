    Performance Study of the IceCube Upgrade Camera System
    ICRC 2025 C. Rott, M. Park, M. Jansson, G. Iverson, S. Choi (for the IceCube Collaboration)
    arXiv:2507.18525 — Instrumentation and Methods for Astrophysics
    Abstract

    The IceCube Upgrade Camera System is a calibration framework for measuring the optical properties of Antarctic ice with cameras and LEDs deployed on D-Egg and mDOM modules. These images can be used to study both local refrozen ice and bulk ice between strings, while also helping constrain module positions. This work compares classical maximum-likelihood and neural-network-based image analysis methods using a dedicated simulation pipeline.

    Generative AI for IceCube Observatory
    Talk
    103rd Spring Meeting of the Korean Physical Society — Daejeon
    Abstract

    This talk presents JANUS, a framework based on conditional diffusion and flow-matching models for IceCube neutrino event simulation and reconstruction. The model learns to generate realistic Cherenkov photon hit patterns conditioned on particle-level quantities, and to reconstruct event properties such as energy and direction from observed detector signals. Both tasks are unified within a single generative modeling framework trained on simulated IceCube data.

    Mixture Density Networks for Fast Detector Response Simulation in SWGO
    Poster
    103rd Spring Meeting of the Korean Physical Society — Daejeon
    Abstract

    This poster presents a Mixture Density Network (MDN) approach for fast simulation of SWGO detector responses. The model is trained on simulated PMT signals from air-shower events and learns the conditional distribution of detector outputs given shower parameters, enabling rapid sampling without full Monte Carlo simulation. Results on signal fidelity and inference speed are compared against baseline methods.

    Investigation of IGZO-Based 3D Vertical Channel Transistors and Physics-Informed Neural Network Modeling for Next-Generation DRAM
    Poster ★ Best Presentation
    33rd Korean Conference on Semiconductors — Jeongseon
    Abstract

    This poster investigates the electrical characteristics of IGZO-based 3D vertical-channel transistors proposed for next-generation DRAM architectures. A physics-informed neural network (PINN) is developed to model device behavior by incorporating semiconductor physics equations as constraints in the loss function. The approach enables accurate and efficient extraction of device parameters compared to conventional simulation methods.

    Generative AI for Cosmological Simulation and Manifold Representation
    Poster
    102nd Fall Meeting of the Korean Physical Society — Gwangju
    Abstract

    This poster presents an early version of GENESIS, an optimal-transport flow-matching model trained on the CAMELS dataset for dark matter density field emulation. The model conditions on cosmological parameters and its outputs are evaluated by comparing power spectra and halo mass distributions against reference simulations. Manifold-level representations of the generated fields are also examined to probe the structure learned by the model.

    Performance Study of the IceCube Upgrade Camera System
    Poster
    39th International Cosmic Ray Conference (ICRC 2025) — Geneva, Switzerland
    Abstract

    This poster presents OPTICUS, a hybrid CNN–Vision Transformer model for analyzing simulated images from the IceCube Upgrade camera system. The model is trained to estimate scattering lengths of bulk ice and refrozen hole ice from calibration images, achieving percent-level accuracy on both targets. Results are compared with classical maximum-likelihood methods across a range of ice conditions.

    Modeling the Relationship between PMT Response and Air-Shower Particles for SWGO
    Poster
    101st Spring Meeting of the Korean Physical Society — Daejeon
    Abstract

    This poster investigates the relationship between PMT response signals and air-shower particle properties in the SWGO detector using simulation data. A generative model is developed to learn the conditional distribution of PMT outputs given shower parameters such as energy, arrival direction, and core position. The approach is intended to support fast and accurate detector-response emulation for SWGO analysis pipelines.

    Development of a Deep Learning Model for Analyzing Antarctic Ice Optical Properties
    Poster Merit Award
    101st Fall Meeting of the Korean Physical Society — Yeosu
    Abstract

    This poster presents a deep-learning approach for measuring the optical properties of Antarctic ice from simulated images produced by the IceCube Upgrade camera system. CNN and Vision Transformer models are trained to estimate scattering and absorption lengths from simulated camera exposures of both bulk and refrozen hole ice. The work demonstrates that neural-network-based methods can automate and accelerate optical calibration of the IceCube detector.

    Study of Optical Properties in Antarctic Ice Using Simulated Images
    Poster
    100th Spring Meeting of the Korean Physical Society — Daejeon
    Abstract

    This poster presents an early study of Antarctic ice optical-property estimation using simulated images from the IceCube Upgrade camera system. A CNN model is developed to predict ice scattering and absorption parameters from camera exposures, with training and evaluation conducted on a dedicated Monte Carlo simulation pipeline. The results demonstrate the feasibility of data-driven optical calibration for the IceCube Upgrade.

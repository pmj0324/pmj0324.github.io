Bio

I am an undergraduate student at Sungkyunkwan University, majoring in physics and electrical engineering, interested in applying neural networks and generative AI to cosmology and astroparticle physics.

I began my research in Prof. Carsten Rott's group, developing CNN and Vision Transformer models to predict optical properties of Antarctic ice from simulated IceCube Upgrade camera images. This work resulted in a proceedings paper at ICRC 2025.

I then joined Prof. Chang Dong Rho's group, where I develop flow matching and diffusion models for IceCube neutrino event simulation and reconstruction, and diffusion models and Mixture Density Networks for SWGO tank simulation — work recognized with a Best Presentation Award at the 4th K-Neutrino Symposium (2025).

For SWGO, I also work with Dr. Ian Watson's group at the University of Seoul on a transformer-based primary-particle reconstruction model, investigating position embedding strategies for detector arrays.

In parallel, I independently develop GENESIS — an optimal-transport flow matching framework for cosmological multifield simulation and parameter inference.

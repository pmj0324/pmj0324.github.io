Research

Machine learning for astro-particle physics experiments

I work on applying modern machine learning methods — including diffusion models, flow matching, CNNs, and transformers — to large-scale detector arrays such as IceCube, HAWC, and SWGO.

IceCube Upgrade Camera System — University of Utah
Undergraduate Researcher · Prof. Carsten Rott · Aug 2023 – Feb 2025
  • Developed CNN and Vision Transformer pipelines for sensor calibration and optical property estimation in the IceCube Upgrade camera system.
  • Contributed to performance studies of the camera system for characterizing Antarctic ice optical properties.

Related work: Performance Study of the IceCube Upgrade Camera System (ICRC 2025).

Generative Models for IceCube Signals — SKKU
Undergraduate Researcher · Prof. Chang Dong Rho · May 2024 – Present
  • Developing score-based generative models for synthesizing PMT charge and timing signals in IceCube.
  • Building flow-matching models to generate detector signals conditioned on neutrino characteristics.
SWGO and Large-Array Detectors — University of Seoul
Undergraduate Researcher · Dr. Ian Watson · Jun 2024 – Present
  • Contributing transformer-based models for inferring primary particle properties from large-array detector time-series data.
  • Developing diffusion and flow-matching models to improve signal synthesis and reconstruction accuracy on SWGO simulation data.

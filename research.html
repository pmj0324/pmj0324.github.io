GENESIS — Multi-field cosmological simulation and inference

Ongoing
Computational Cosmology · CAMELS Multifield Dataset · 2025 – Present

GENESIS is a unified project for multi-field cosmological generation and parameter inference in the CAMELS simulation suite.

Project
GENESIS builds a conditional generative model for dark matter, gas, and temperature fields across the astrophysical and cosmological parameter space of CAMELS.
Generation
The model learns a probability-flow vector field with a flow-matching loss, then integrates that field to generate multi-field maps conditioned on the target parameter vector.
Inference
Because the same learned flow can be evaluated through the change-of-variables formula, we are studying training-free likelihood-based inference of astrophysical and cosmological parameters from generated-model probabilities.

IceCube OPTICUS Camera System

Completed
University of Utah · Advisor: Prof. Carsten Rott · Aug 2023 – Feb 2025

OPTICUS studies calibration for the IceCube Upgrade through camera-based measurements of Antarctic ice and module geometry.

System
Nearly 2,000 cameras and LEDs on D-Egg and mDOM modules record transmission and reflection images across the Upgrade volume.
Goal
These images help characterize refrozen drill-hole ice, bulk ice between strings, and the positions of the optical modules.
Method
I compare classical maximum-likelihood and neural-network analysis methods using images from a dedicated simulation pipeline.

JANUS — Generative modeling for IceCube signals

Ongoing
SKKU · Advisor: Prof. Chang Dong Rho · May 2024 – Feb 2026

JANUS applies generative modeling to IceCube event simulation and reconstruction in a single framework.

Physics
IceCube reconstructs quantities such as energy, direction, and interaction vertex from sparse and noisy Cherenkov-photon signals in Antarctic ice.
Model
JANUS develops conditional score-based diffusion and flow-matching models trained on simulated IceCube detector responses.
Objective
The same framework is designed to generate detector signals from particle-level conditions and to infer event properties from observed photon data.
